6 · Questions worth sitting with

This isn't a list of recommendations — these are the questions the data raises.

  1. Is the free gummy meant to be a brand entry point, an email-list builder, or a profitable acquisition channel? The current setup performs differently against each goal.
  2. Does Klaviyo show meaningful email engagement from these 547 contacts? If they're opening emails and clicking but not converting on Shopify in 60 days, the LTV may exist beyond this window.
  3. Are there past free gummy customers (acquired in Jan/Feb) who are subscribers today? If yes, the funnel works on a longer timescale than this report can see. If no, the pattern likely holds.
  4. Is there an upsell/OTO between the free gummy click and the order confirmation? Of the 547, the ones who bought a paid product mostly did so by going back to the home page — the funnel itself doesn't seem to ask for an upgrade.
  5. What's the actual landed cost of the free gummy? The CSV says $1 COGS + $7.40 fulfillment. If real-world fulfillment is higher (e.g. $10+), the loss per customer is larger.
  6. Of the 9 paid repeaters, 6 bought the $9.99 100mg bag — essentially "the same product but slightly bigger." Is this a buying signal that suggests a different SKU should be the upsell, or that customers are happy with the free version and never trade up?

7 · A few directions you could explore

Possible moves, ordered by reversibility. Not prescriptions — just things the data points toward.

Low-risk, easy to test

  • Add a one-time-offer (OTO) page after the free gummy add-to-cart. If even 5% of customers take a $20-30 upsell, per-customer revenue goes from $1 to $2-3.
  • Watch what older cohorts do. Wait 60 more days and re-run this analysis. If the W11-W14 cohorts (now 4-8 weeks mature) start showing subscription conversion at month 3-4, the funnel works on a longer timescale than this window can see.
  • Audit Klaviyo for FG-segment email engagement. If email open/click rates are healthy but Shopify doesn't show conversion, the LTV is being captured by email but not visible in this report.

Medium-risk, would change the funnel shape

  • Cap free-gummy spend. Instead of pulling it, hold it at a level where the loss is acceptable as a brand cost (e.g., $50/day instead of ~$95/day) and free up budget for tests on other angles.
  • Test "free + ship + paid product" — same offer with a paid product attached at the cart step.
  • Repoint the free-gummy ads at a different LP that pre-sells before the free claim, similar to the advertorial structure used for ADHD/PANDAS/Anxiety.

Higher-risk, only if the data above is decisive

  • Pause the free-gummy funnel entirely for 30 days. Watch what happens to total Shopify revenue. If it stays flat, the funnel wasn't producing measurable LTV. If it drops, it was — and you can quantify by how much.

The data above is what's observable in the 60-day Shopify window. None of these directions are obvious wins or obvious mistakes — they each have reasons to try and reasons to wait. The right call depends on context the report doesn't have: brand positioning, email LTV from Klaviyo, what other funnels are already in motion.
