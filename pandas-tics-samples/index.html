20 survivor-voice native image ads for Facebook | Leads to: "I Spent $50,000 to Fix My Motor Tics — This Is the Only Thing That Worked"

Survivor-Voice Confession

The narrator on the landing page is an adult who had PANDAS as a teenager. The body of these ads keeps that voice — first person, recounting their own motor tics, their own $50,000 chase with their parents, their own recovery.

The audience is still parents searching for answers for their own child. What makes the testimony land is hearing the future their child could have, narrated by someone who already got there.

Every ad anchors on three things from the on-image headline: my motor tics, $50,000 burned, and "the one thing that worked." Meta Headlines extend that reveal — they never pivot to naming PANDAS. The funnel does the naming. The ad only sells the click.
